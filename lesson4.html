The Gift of Emotions

üíé Emotional Intensity as Wisdom

The pre-menstrual phase can be very emotionally intense. Some people may find they become more decisive and better able to say what they like and don't like. Some find they're more comfortable expressing their opinions during the days before menstruation starts.

This emotional volatility isn't something to fear or suppress - it's a good sign! Your emotions are telling you what you truly care about. The ability to express opinions and desires is self-empowering for all individuals.

üåü Reframing "Difficult" Emotions

Many girls and women have been conditioned to believe that having strong emotions during their cycle makes them "difficult" or "intolerable." This is not true. Your pre-menstrual emotions are valuable messengers showing you what matters most to you.

Rather than viewing this phase as something to endure, see it as an opportunity for authentic expression and creative breakthrough. Your emotions during this time can fuel incredible artistic and creative work.

Creative Expression Activities

Channel Your Emotional Energy into Creative Flow

Freeform Dance Practice

Purpose: Expressing Creativity & Honoring Emotions

Dance is one of the most powerful ways to express pre-menstrual emotions. Your body knows exactly how to move to release tension and express feelings.

Let your emotions guide the movement - there are no "correct" movements, only authentic expression.

Step-by-Step Instructions

1

Put on music that matches your current emotional state - upbeat, slow, intense, or gentle.

2

Stand in a comfortable space where you can move freely without worrying about hitting anything.

3

Close your eyes and feel the music in your body. Notice where the rhythm resonates.

4

Let your body move however it wants - no rules, no "correct" movements, just authentic expression.

5

Express whatever emotions you're feeling: let anger move through your arms and legs, let sadness flow through your spine, let joy lift you up.

6

Dance for 10-20 minutes, letting your emotions completely guide the movement.

7

End by standing still and feeling the energy in your body. Notice any shifts in how you feel.

Emotional Journaling Practice

Purpose: Honoring Your Inner Voice & Emotional Wisdom

Writing during the pre-menstrual phase can be incredibly revealing. Your emotions often carry important messages about what you need and what matters to you.

Step-by-Step Instructions

1

Set aside 15-20 minutes in a quiet space where you won't be interrupted.

2

Write the date and current moon phase at the top of your page.

3

Ask yourself: "What am I feeling right now?" and write without editing or censoring.

4

Explore: "What is this emotion trying to tell me?" Allow yourself to be curious.

5

Write about: "What do I need right now?" Be specific and honest.

6

List things you appreciate about yourself today, especially your emotional courage.

7

End with an affirmation or intention for the coming days.

Emotional Journaling: Provides a safe space to explore your inner world without judgment. Your pre-menstrual insights often contain profound wisdom about what you need to thrive.
